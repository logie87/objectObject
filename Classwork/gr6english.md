# GRADE 6 ENGLISH LANGUAGE ARTS
## STORY & CHARACTER SNAPSHOT – 1 WEEK ASSIGNMENT
Teacher + Student Markdown Edition

---

## SECTION A: TEACHER OVERVIEW

### Title
**Story & Character Snapshot (ELA 6)**

### Timeframe
**1 week**

### Purpose

A short, focused assignment to assess how students can:

- Read an appropriate text (short novel, short story, or excerpt) independently or with support.
- Identify key story elements: character, setting, important events, message.
- Express their understanding in clear, simple writing.
- Make a personal connection to the text.
- Share their thinking in a short, low-stress presentation.

Designed to be:

- Doable in one week.
- Easy to scaffold or extend.
- Aligned with BC ELA 6 goals: comprehend & connect, create & communicate, reflect.

### Core Components (Suggested)

1. **Reading + Log / Check-in** – completion  
2. **Story Snapshot Sheet (written)** – 25 marks  
3. **Character & Connection Task** – 15 marks  
4. **Mini Book Share (Presentation)** – 10 marks  

**Total Suggested:** 50 marks (adjust as needed)

### Recommended Week Flow

- **Day 1:** Introduce task, students choose text (or use class text), start reading.
- **Day 2:** Reading time + start Story Snapshot Sheet.
- **Day 3:** Finish Snapshot Sheet, start Character & Connection Task.
- **Day 4:** Finish written work, prepare Mini Book Share.
- **Day 5:** Book Shares and final hand-in.

### Text Options

- Whole-class text (short novel or story), or  
- Teacher-approved choices at suitable reading levels, with support as needed.

### Submission Expectations

Students submit:

- Simple **Reading Log** (or teacher check-in).
- **Story Snapshot Sheet** (all sections complete).
- **Character & Connection Task**.
- Completion of **Mini Book Share**.

### Marking Focus (Teacher Summary)

**Story Snapshot Sheet (25)**  
- Clear summary and details.  
- Correct identification of main character, setting, key event, message.  
- Simple, readable writing.

**Character & Connection Task (15)**  
- Shows understanding of character.  
- Makes a personal or real-world connection.  
- Uses details from the text.

**Mini Book Share (10)**  
- Names text and author.  
- Shares main idea and one connection or opinion.  
- Clear, brief, and appropriate for Grade 6.

### IEP / Differentiation Options

You may:

- Allow oral or scribed responses.
- Accept bullet points instead of full sentences if explained by the student.
- Use audio, shared reading, or partner reading.
- Shorten length expectations.
- Prioritize comprehension and engagement over conventions.
- Extend by inviting deeper theme analysis for advanced students.

All students follow the same structure:
**Read → Understand → Connect → Share**

## SECTION B: STUDENT ASSIGNMENT

### Title
**Story & Character Snapshot – Grade 6**

### Time
**1 week**

You will:

1. Read a story or short novel (your teacher will approve it).  
2. Fill out the **Story Snapshot Sheet**.  
3. Do ONE **Character & Connection** task.  
4. Share a **2-minute Book Talk** with the class.

Your teacher will give you the due dates.

---

## PART 1: READING & LOG

1. Choose your book or use the class story.
2. Read a bit each day.

Each time you read, write:
- Date  
- Pages or chapters read  

Example:  
`Mon – Ch. 1–2 – Met the main character and saw where they live.`

This shows steady reading.

---

## PART 2: STORY SNAPSHOT SHEET (25 MARKS)

Answer the questions below. Keep it on one page if you can.

### 1. Basic Info

- **Title:**  
- **Author:**  

### 2. Quick Summary (3–5 sentences)

Tell what the story is mostly about. Do not retell every detail.

### 3. Setting (2–3 sentences)

- Where does the story happen?  
- When does it happen (if we know)?  
- Is there something important about the place or time?

### 4. Main Character (3–5 sentences)

- Who is the main character?  
- What are they like?  
- What is one problem, choice, or challenge they face?

### 5. Important Moment (3–4 sentences)

Choose ONE important moment.

Explain:
- What happened.  
- Why it is important to the character or the story.

**Marked On (25):**

- You show you understand the story.
- You include some clear details.
- Your writing is mostly clear and easy to read.

---

## PART 3: CHARACTER & CONNECTION TASK (15 MARKS)

Choose ONE option.

### Option A: Character & Me

Write **5–7 sentences**:

- One way you are similar to the main character OR different from them.
- Use at least one example from the story.
- Explain how this helps you understand the character.

### Option B: Character Map

Make a simple character map with:

- 3–5 words that describe the main character.
- 3–5 short notes from the story that prove those words.
- 2–3 sentences: Would you be friends with this character? Why or why not?

### Option C: Letter to the Character

Write **6–8 sentences** to the main character:

- Say what you noticed about them.
- Ask 1–2 questions.
- Give one piece of advice or support.

**Marked On (15):**

- Shows you understand the character.
- Uses details from the book.
- Shows your own thinking.

---

## PART 4: MINI BOOK SHARE (10 MARKS)

About **2 minutes**.

In your Book Share, include:

1. Title and author.  
2. Who the story is mainly about.  
3. One or two sentences about what happens (no big spoilers).  
4. One thing you liked, noticed, or connected to.  
5. If you recommend it to other Grade 6 students, and why.

You may use:
- A small cue card.
- The book cover as a visual.

**Marked On (10):**

- Includes all parts.
- Clear and on topic.
- Shows you know your book.
